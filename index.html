The 2008 Great Recession and its after-impact

The Great Recession was the general economic decline observed in world markets around the end of the first decade of the 21st century. The International Monetary Fund concluded that it was the worst global recession since World War II. The Great Recession applies to both the United States recession and the ensuing global recession. The economic incident began when the United States housing market went from boom to bust and large amounts of mortgage-backed securities and derivatives lost significant value. It was related as the cause of the United States financial crisis of 2007-2008 and subprime mortgage crisis of 2007-2009.
